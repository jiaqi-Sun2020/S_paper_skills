# Journal Styles

Use this reference after the manuscript's scientific content is known. Style cannot compensate for a missing central claim or unsupported evidence.

## Nature And Nature-Family

- Lead with broad relevance before specialist mechanism.
- Make the advance legible to readers outside the immediate subfield.
- Use precise, restrained significance language.
- Cut detail before compressing it into dense sentences.
- Flag weak general significance instead of overstating it.

## Physical Review Letters

PRL-style polishing should make the paper feel like one decisive physics result.

- Put the physical question and central result early.
- Keep the introduction short: context, gap, result, why it matters.
- Compress methods to what is needed to trust the result, with details deferred when appropriate.
- Make each figure serve the central claim.
- Prefer one strong through-line over a list of contributions.
- Use cautious novelty language; never claim priority without evidence.

## Physical Review A

PRA-style polishing should make the mechanism, model, and reproducibility clear.

- State the quantum system, state space, operators, measurement model, assumptions, and parameter regimes explicitly.
- Keep notation stable across abstract, body, equations, and captions.
- Separate demonstrated results from proposed extensions.
- Preserve enough implementation and protocol detail for an expert reader to reproduce the calculation or simulation.
- Use limitations as part of the physics interpretation, not as a perfunctory ending.

## PRL Versus PRA Choice

- Prefer PRL logic when the manuscript has a short, surprising, broadly important physics result supported by compact evidence.
- Prefer PRA logic when the paper's strength is a method, protocol, reconstruction model, numerical study, or detailed quantum-information analysis that needs space for assumptions and checks.
- If the evidence is still developing, polish in PRA style by default and flag what would be required for PRL compression.
